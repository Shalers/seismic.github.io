Evaluation of Shale Gas Reservoir Using Seismic and Non-Seismic data

Abstract

Shale gas refers to natural gas that is trapped within shale formations. Shales are fine-grained sedimentary rocks that can be rich sources of petroleum and natural gas.

Shale reservoirs can be complex and present many challenges, including identification, mapping, and staying within sweet spots; determining well locations and spacing; optimizing drilling; enhancing completion and stimulation strategies; identifying operational efficiencies; and respecting environmental sensitivities and regulatory issues.

Our task is to, determine the feasibility for exploration and exploitation of reservoir using seismic and non-seismic data sets to study its impact on oil and gas industries which can be achieved through quantitative evaluation of Shale gas reservoir and designing a model based upon seismic and non-seismic data.

Prediction of Reservoir Pressure, Fluid Identification and Production Rate of a shale gas reservoir are the results we get from the project.
